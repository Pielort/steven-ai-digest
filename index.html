AI news is cheap. Signal is scarce.

StevenAiDigest is a daily brief for builders, operators, and investors who want advantage — not entertainment. Most coverage is optimized for virality: big claims, thin evidence, and a permanent state of “this changes everything.” That creates noise, not decisions.

Here, every edition is written in a finance‑grade style: thesis → evidence → implication → action. I’m explicit about what changed, what’s measurable, where the uncertainty is, and what a serious operator should do next.

Launching StevenAiDigest

Most people follow AI like spectators: headlines, vibes, and hot takes. That’s fine if your goal is to be entertained — but it’s a terrible way to build product, run a team, or allocate capital. Advantage comes from knowing what changed at the capability layer, which constraints still bind, and what is now practical.

StevenAiDigest is built for operators. I’ll publish four updates per day: (1) a breakthrough/model brief, (2) a practical workflow or tool that reduces friction, (3) an adjacent innovation that shifts the cost curve, and (4) a market/incentive lens that explains who wins and why. The goal is simple: reduce noise, increase signal, and turn “AI news” into decisions.
