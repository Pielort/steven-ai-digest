AI Enters the Research+Execution Era: Proof-Grade Reasoning Meets Agentic Cost Curves

Thesis: The frontier is shifting from “chat that sounds smart” to systems that produce verifiable work. Google DeepMind’s Gemini Deep Think research agent (Aletheia) is pushing into publishable, research-level math and cross-disciplinary problem solving, while Alibaba positions Qwen 3.5 as an agentic model optimized for executing complex tasks at materially lower cost. The emerging operator edge is verification + execution, not eloquence.

Evidence:

  • DeepMind (Feb 11, 2026): Gemini Deep Think is being applied to professional research problems; DeepMind describes a math research agent (“Aletheia”) that iteratively generates, verifies, and revises solutions and can admit failure (improving researcher efficiency). DeepMind reports the system reaches up to ~90% on IMO-ProofBench Advanced as inference-time compute scales and cites papers + arXiv work enabled by varying degrees of autonomy. (DeepMind blog)
  • Alibaba / Qwen 3.5 (reported Feb 16, 2026): Alibaba frames Qwen 3.5 for “agentic AI,” claiming it is ~60% cheaper and ~8x more capable at processing large workloads than the prior version, with “visual agentic capabilities” to operate across mobile/desktop apps. (IndexBox summary of Reuters)

Implication:

  1. Research becomes a pipeline: “Advisor + verifier” loops (generate → check → revise) are becoming the default way to use frontier models for hard problems. This compresses time-to-proof, time-to-literature synthesis, and time-to-experiment design.
  2. Enterprise agents move from demo to unit economics: Cost-per-task matters more than cost-per-token. If Qwen’s claimed efficiency holds in real deployments, agentic automation expands from “high-value, low-volume” workflows to “high-volume, boring work.”
  3. Verification is the moat: The best stacks will be those that can prove correctness (or detect uncertainty) and route work accordingly (human review, tool-based checking, or multi-model consensus).

Action:

  • Operators: Standardize a “research loop” playbook: retrieval + tool-based checks + explicit refutation prompts + versioned artifacts. Treat it like CI/CD for thinking.
  • Builders: Add verifiers (symbolic checks, unit tests, graders) as first-class components; measure success as verified outputs per dollar, not subjective quality.
  • Investors: Re-rate teams that own verification workflows, data + eval infrastructure, and distribution into enterprise systems; model advantage alone is becoming transient.
