Executive take

AI infrastructure partnerships scale to unprecedented levels. Multi-gigawatt data center deals redefine compute capacity planning. Energy procurement becomes strategic capability for AI scaling.

Item 1: OpenAI and Oracle announce 4.5 GW data center expansion

Thesis: AI compute capacity scales through infrastructure partnerships, not just internal buildout.

Evidence: OpenAI and Oracle announce joint venture in July 2025 to add 4.5 GW of US data center capacity.

Implication: AI infrastructure becomes specialized asset class requiring partnership models for scaling.

Action: Evaluate infrastructure partnerships for AI scaling rather than assuming internal capacity buildout.

Sources: GM Insights: OpenAI-Oracle Data Center Partnership

Item 2: Stargate program expands with massive capacity additions

Thesis: Government-backed AI infrastructure programs accelerate capacity deployment.

Evidence: 4.5 GW addition represents significant expansion of existing Stargate program capacity.

Implication: Public-private partnerships become essential for AI infrastructure at national scale.

Action: Monitor government infrastructure programs for AI capacity allocation opportunities.

Sources: GM Insights: Stargate Program Expansion

Item 3: Energy scale defines AI infrastructure competitiveness

Thesis: Power procurement capability determines AI scaling potential more than hardware availability.

Evidence: Multi-gigawatt deals require energy procurement at utility scale, not just data center construction.

Implication: AI companies must develop energy procurement as core competency alongside technical capabilities.

Action: Develop energy procurement strategy as integral part of AI infrastructure planning.

Sources: Enki AI: Energy Procurement Analysis

Item 4: Behind-the-meter generation becomes AI infrastructure requirement

Thesis: Grid independence becomes competitive advantage for AI data centers.

Evidence: 33% of planned data center projects include behind-the-meter generation, primarily natural gas.

Implication: Energy resilience becomes as important as compute density for AI infrastructure.

Action: Evaluate on-site generation options for AI infrastructure to ensure operational continuity.

Sources: Enki AI: Behind-the-Meter Generation Trends
